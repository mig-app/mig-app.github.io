# mig, three surfaces
How the app, the site and the youtube channel relate, and what goes on each.
Written 2026-07-19. The plan came out of a judged pass over three competing
strategies; what survived is below.

## the one description
Name: **MiG: Russian Tutor**
Tagline (27 chars, fits the app store subtitle cap): `russian, a little at a time`

> миг: a blink, an instant. mig is a free russian vocabulary app: 1363 words across 27 themed sets, a1 to b1, plus everyday sentences. four ways through any set: блиц to read it at speed, карточки to flip through it, повторяй to say each word aloud and be scored on your device, слушай to listen hands free on a walk. no accounts, no ads, nothing collected, works offline. built by one person who is learning russian too, and the russian you hear is a machine voice. small sessions, a little at a time, until the russian starts to feel familiar.

Use this paragraph verbatim in the app store description, the site, and the
youtube about box. One string, not three variants that drift.

## what each surface is for
- app: the only surface that remembers you. streak, level, hidden mastery, the слово дня widget, offline, lock screen playback, csv and json import, and a mic that listens. anything involving your progress belongs here and nowhere else.
- site: the front door and the no-install trial. блиц, карточки, and повторяй run in the browser on the full 1363 words. it holds the roadmap, privacy, support, and about, and it is the canonical home of the description paragraph. it is the only surface a link can point at from anywhere.
- youtube: the passive surface. a screen you are not holding. the kitchen, the walk, the bus, a tv nobody is touching. it is also where search lives, because people type russian a1 vocabulary listening into youtube and not into the app store.
- the allocation rule, which is the whole structure: each mode gets exactly one home per context. слушай lives in the app and on youtube and never on the site, because long audio in a browser tab that sleeps is a worse version of what the app already does with the screen off. повторяй lives in the app and on the site and never on youtube, because a video cannot hear you and one that cut to correct after a pause would be theater. блиц lives in the app and on the site and not on youtube, for the reason given in the formats section. карточки is not a video format at all. this gives every surface something the others structurally cannot have.
- the mirror rule all three source plans missed: no state on youtube, ever. no scoring, no quizzes, no streaks, nothing that remembers. once all three surfaces carry the same 1363 words, memory is the app's entire remaining claim, and one scored quiz video would take it.
- site to youtube: one line and one link, below the modes, after video one exists and not before. no embed grid. youtube already does hosting for free and the site's speed is part of the pitch.
- site to app: the existing app store cta, unchanged.
- app to youtube: two lines total, the about screen and слушай's finished state. the app must not become a channel promo.
- youtube to site: every description links the exact set that video just read, using the ?set= param added before video one. mig-app.github.io/flash/?set=a1-greetings-and-introductions is a click. the app store is a decision. ask for the click first, always, and keep the site link above the store link in every description permanently.
- youtube to app: framed by what video cannot do, which is remember. progress, streaks, offline, your own imported sets. never framed as download for more content, because the content is the same 1363 words everywhere.
- the handoff, one fixed line in every description, never rewritten: video is for when your hands are full. the app is for when they are not.
- channel art carries the site url, not the app store link. one destination from the banner keeps the site as the hub rather than a redirect.
- the correction loop, with a named cadence so it is not aspirational: comments stay open, and they are free native-speaker review of the stress the synthesizer gets wrong. read them once a month, in the same session as the batch, and never between. one line in the about box, said once and never repeated in a video: corrections welcome, they go into the app. a stress fix goes into the romanized field and flows to all four renderers in one commit. for a project that collects nothing, this is the only feedback channel that exists.
- descriptions are generated, never hand written. line one is set name, level, word count. line two is what the video does. then the site link, the store link, the disclosure, and generated chapters. the moment a video needs writing it stops being free and the channel dies at video nine with a folder of drafts.

## the formats

### слушай listen · 12 to 18 minutes, one set per video, 27 sets in the library
- generated from the json: **yes**
- on screen: one still card per beat on the ground color. english small and dim above, russian large in near-white, transliteration below in the mono face with its stressed vowel in near-white against the rest of the line dimmed. set name and level bottom left, position (18 / 47) bottom right, a hairline creeping across the bottom as the set advances. nothing else moves. no music, no progress bar, nothing that asks to be watched. it must read at arm's length on a kitchen counter and be entirely ignorable. two design decisions the source plans got wrong: the stress mark is not oxblood, because style.css line 373 states the shipped rule verbatim, the gloss is small, muted, never the accent, and tokens.css says the accent means confirm and advance, which a diacritic is not. brightness carries the mark instead. and the mark sits on the transliteration only, never on the cyrillic, because the acute already lives in that string and mapping it back across digraphs breaks: i tested a naive vowel-group map against all 1197 marked entries and 31 run out of range (пойдём, семья, пятьдесят, объяснять). cyrillic marking is a v2 with a hand-checked table, not a v1.
- made by: one node script, one afternoon plus a weekend, reused by both generated formats. it reads mig-ios/json directly, which is the source, not the two downstream copies. audio: say -v Milena for russian, say -v Samantha for english, one aiff each, durations measured with afinfo (ffmpeg and ffprobe are not installed on this machine; brew install ffmpeg is step zero and afinfo covers duration in the meantime). frames: one html card rendered against the site's own tokens.css and style.css, screenshotted by headless chrome at 1920x1080, forced to data-theme dark or some renders come out white. assembly: ffmpeg concat demuxer, file/duration pairs, -tune stillimage. chapters fall out of the same timeline json. the structure per card follows the app's real loop, which every source plan misread: ListenPlayer.swift makes each pass an english-then-russian pair, repeated twice by default, 0.35 between languages and 0.6 after the pair. the video adds one thing the app does not, a 1.6 second say-it-back pause before the russian repeats, which is why пауза is not a separate format here. it was 27 near-duplicate videos differing only in silence length, competing with /repeat/ for the same intent. folding it in costs one line in the timing table. measured on real output (avg russian 0.63s, english 0.72s over 12 greetings cards) that is 4.7 seconds per card per pass. passes are then derived, not asserted: passes = round(900 / (cards x 4.7)), clamped 2 to 6, so a 47-card set runs 4 passes at 14.7 minutes, a 90-card set runs 2 at 14.1, a 31-card set runs 6 at 14.6. every video lands in the same band without a range that the data breaks. entries containing a literal ellipsis (мне ... лет, я из) are skipped from video cards, because a full-frame ellipsis reads as a rendering bug at counter distance.
- honesty: one six second cold-open card, no voiceover, identical in every video and in every description: the audio is a machine voice, apple's compact russian voice, the same one the app uses by default. it puts the stress in the wrong place sometimes. where the romanization carries an accent mark, that is where the stress belongs. every clause there is literally true, which the source wordings were not. the voice is rendered on a mac, not on the viewer's phone, so built into your phone is false in a video. the app lets a learner download an enhanced voice and the site roadmap promises better audio, so the same one the app uses is false too, and by default fixes it. and where there is a mark is scoped deliberately: 166 of 1363 entries carry no acute. the good news is that the hole is far smaller than the judges thought. i counted cyrillic vowels rather than latin ones and only 9 unmarked entries are genuinely multisyllabic: неровный, отчёт, где-то, кто-то, что-то, не за что, and three fragments. отчёт is free because ё is always stressed, which is a one-line rule in the generator. неровный and the four compounds are fifteen minutes of work. fix those nine before video one, then the claim holds across the whole word corpus.

### слово дня word of the day · 30 to 45 seconds, vertical 1080x1920
- generated from the json: **yes**
- on screen: the same card, one word, filling the frame. english, russian, transliteration with the stressed vowel bright. the set it came from in small dim type at the bottom. spoken once, a pause, once more. no burned captions, no zoom, no countdown.
- made by: the same script, a vertical stylesheet, run in batch. DailyPick.swift is a pure deterministic function of the day number with a salt, so the sequence is known a month ahead and the short and the widget show the same word on the same day. it is an existing feature on another surface rather than a new editorial job. batch fifteen per session, not thirty: at one short a week a thirty-batch grows the queue by seventeen a month forever and wastes half the session.
- honesty: the transliteration corrects the voice in the same frame. one line in the fixed description, nothing more: machine voice, the accent mark shows where the stress belongs. never presented as pronunciation instruction, only as the word, its meaning, and where the stress falls.

### заметка a note · 2 to 4 minutes, at most one a month and often none
- generated from the json: **no, this one needs a person**
- on screen: a still frame in the site's own type on the dark ground, one line of text at a time, or a plain screen recording of the app. no face, no camera. voice only.
- made by: the only format that needs a person. recorded on a phone in a quiet room, one take from notes, cut in whatever is free. thirty to ninety minutes end to end if the point is already clear. this is also the fallback: it needs no pipeline at all, so if the renderer is not done it can publish first.
- honesty: the hard rule that makes a human format safe here: devin speaks english, about his own software and his own practice, and pronounces no russian at all, ever. if a russian word must appear it is the app saying it with the written form on screen, which makes it a demo of the synthesizer rather than a model to imitate. no grammar, no instruction, no correcting the viewer. the subjects are his: why mastery is tracked and hidden, why there are no accounts, why the voice is a machine, why six modes were retired, what he got wrong this month and what he changed because of it. a learner's month is a frame that cannot be falsified, and it is the one position no polished channel can take.

## the first videos
1. слушай · greetings & introductions, a1, 47 words, 4 passes, about 15 minutes. the first video, so it carries the full cold open and sets the template. chapters generated per word. description links flash/?set=a1-greetings-and-introductions and the app store. its real job is to prove the render end to end, not to perform. prerequisites, all small and all before upload: fix the nine unmarked multisyllables, add ?set= to the three browser modes, delete the native-checked comment from phrases.js.
2. слово дня · спасибо, about 35 seconds, vertical. first of a batch of fifteen generated the same session. spasíbo, stress bright on the и, said twice.
3. слушай · food & meals, a1, 48 words, about 15 minutes. deliberately boring, and that is the test: by now the pipeline is a command and the only human work is the upload. if this one costs more than twenty minutes of clicking, the plan is wrong and the batch size needs to come down again.
4. слово дня, second short from the same batch, scheduled a week after the first. no new work at all.
5. заметка · why mig hides your score, about 3 minutes. the first human video and the proof a person is behind the channel. it explains the most distinctive decision in the app: mastery is tracked and never shown, because the number would be subjective and would start lying to you. contingency, and it matters more than the order: if the renderer is not finished by week three, this publishes first and everything shifts. and if 1.1 has not cleared review, do not announce it here, do not hedge with coming soon, publish слушай · numbers, time & dates instead. never announce on youtube something a reviewer can still reject.
6. not in the first five, and this is a decision against all three source plans: everyday sentences. two of them made it video three and called it the most searchable. i checked the file. a1-sentences.json has zero stress marks across all 68 entries and uses a different phonetic convention (zavut, utra) than the accented one the word sets use (spasíbo), and it is absent from mig-sets.js entirely, so there is nothing to link. the honesty mechanic cannot hold there and a non-native cannot add 68 stress marks without importing exactly the risk this plan is built to avoid. the sentences wait until someone qualified marks them, and if that never happens they never ship.

## cadence

"one session a month, roughly two hours, and it is the only obligation. that session: render two слушай sets, generate fifteen слово дня, upload and schedule six assets, read the month's comments, close the laptop. published rate: one long video every two weeks, one short a week, заметка only when there is something true to say. six assets is the right batch size for two reasons the source plans missed. first, generation is automated and uploading is not: every asset needs a title, description, chapters, visibility, schedule date, made-for-kids checkbox, and playlist in youtube studio, at roughly two and a half minutes each, so a thirty-three asset afternoon is eighty minutes of pure clicking before a byte moves. second, if it is ever automated, videos.insert costs about 1600 quota units against a default daily quota of 10000, which is six uploads a day. the shrunk batch happens to fit the wall exactly. the weekly appearance is a scheduling artifact, not a weekly obligation, so a bad exam week costs nothing. runway: 27 sets at one a fortnight is about thirteen months, and month fourteen deserves an answer now rather than a panic then. the answer is that the channel ends when the catalog ends, and that is fine. if it should continue, the two cheap continuations are already on disk: a1 complete as one long video, and the sentences once someone qualified has marked the stress. never announce the cadence anywhere, so there is nothing to fall behind on. build phase honesty: the renderer is eight to twenty part-time hours, not an afternoon, and until it exists there is no queue to coast on. that is the one genuinely risky stretch in this plan. if it slips, заметка publishes and nothing is lost."

## what not to do
- no talking head learn russian videos, no grammar explainers, no case tables, no pronunciation tutorials. devin is a learner. it is the most fact-checked genre on russian youtube and the one place a comment section turns hostile. the app already made this decision when падежи was retired. do not quietly re-adopt it on a surface with no code review.
- no блиц videos, and this cuts half the runway two source plans counted on. their own redundancy test kills it: a video cannot score you, so a повторяй video is theater. the same test applies. a silent word list is not passive, it is the opposite, it demands your eyes and gives your ears nothing, so it is blank on a tv, in a car, and on a walk, and at a desk /flash/ is strictly better because it is interactive and does any set from 50 to 1000 wpm with no install. twenty-eight videos that are a worse copy of a page the site already serves is not runway. six months of слушай is a stronger channel than a year of half filler.
- no повторяй video, for the same reason, stated permanently: video should only do what video does, which is play with nobody touching it.
- do not put слушай on the website, and do not put state on youtube. the first deletes the channel's reason to exist. the second deletes the app's.
- do not make a sentences video until the 68 entries carry stress marks in the same convention as the word sets. not marking them yourself. the corpus that has been checked is the corpus that ships.
- do not mark stress on the cyrillic until a mapping table has been hand-checked against all 1363. a confidently wrong mark is strictly worse than no mark, and it is the one failure the honesty pitch cannot survive.
- do not carry checked by native speakers, or any variant, anywhere near youtube. on the site it was one file to fix. in a description it is frozen across a thousand copies. delete the existing one from phrases.js in the same commit as the site edits.
- no roadmap, no coming soon, no promises of any kind in a video or a description. the site already burned itself promising six modes that got retired, and youtube timestamps every promise permanently. the site is currently doing it again at index.html line 62 and lines 86 to 91, about this very plan, with a cta to an empty channel. fix that before publishing, not after.
- no numbered lessons. numbered lessons imply a curriculum, a curriculum implies a teacher, and there isn't one. titles are what the video literally is: mode, set name, level, word count. it is also the only scheme a script can generate, which is why it survives.
- no music bed, no lo-fi loop, no ambient pad, no intro sting. a backing track makes слушай useless as background practice, buries the russian, and adds a licensing tail forever.
- no human russian voice, ever, including devin's own, for a single word. one machine voice, disclosed, is coherent. a mix makes the disclosure incoherent and quietly implies the human parts are authoritative.
- no thumbnails with faces, arrows, circles, all-caps, or shocked expressions. the thumbnail is a frame from the video, generated from the same stylesheet. it will underperform and that is the correct trade. naming the cost is what makes the rule hold in month six.
- no monetization, no sponsorship, no membership, no donation link in the fixed description. the app's entire claim is free, ad-free, collects nothing. one preroll costs more than the revenue, and donations are a roadmap idea, not a thing.
- no daily upload, no shorts treadmill, no fourth surface. no tiktok, no instagram, no second channel. three surfaces is already one more than one part-time person can keep honest. shorts stay at one a week specifically because they are the one format with real algorithmic pull, which is exactly the pressure that turns a quiet channel into a content job.
- do not hand write a single per-video script, intro, or description. if a format cannot be generated, it does not go on a schedule.
- do not build a video editor, a web renderer, or a pipeline app. ffmpeg, headless chrome, the existing tokens.css, one node script. if the tooling takes longer than a weekend past the first working render, stop and publish заметка instead.
- do not generate the whole catalog and dump it. the queue is the asset, and spending it in a week leaves a dead channel with a full library and no reason to return.
- do not respond to a slow first month by making more videos. the correct response is none. the audience for a quiet ad-free vocabulary channel arrives slowly or not at all, and the plan has to make not at all survivable. check once, at video ten, whether this is still worth two hours a month, and be willing to answer no.

## the idea underneath

"the screen corrects the voice: the stress marks already sitting in the json are trustworthy where they exist and the machine tts is not, and video is the only surface where both can be present at once for someone whose hands are full, which turns the app's two real weaknesses, a synthetic voice and a non-native author, into the stated mechanic of the format rather than its apology."
